About Us

Poona Panjarpol Trust is a beacon of compassion nestled in the heart of Pune, Maharashtra. Founded in 1855, our Trust has been dedicated to the welfare, protection, and rehabilitation of cows in distress. From humble beginnings with just a handful of cattle, we have grown into a sprawling sanctuary that today shelters over 1,800+ cows, providing them with love, safety, and care. Our mission transcends animal care — we aim to preserve India's sacred relationship with cows, promote kindness towards all , and educate society about ethical and sustainable living. Our shelter is maintained by a dedicated team of staff, volunteers, and veterinarians who work tirelessly to ensure every animal receives proper food, medical care, and a life of dignity. Beyond animal welfare, Poona Panjarpol Trust actively engages in community development, environmental initiatives, and spiritual services, believing that true progress includes compassion for all beings.

Our Mission

Our mission is rooted in the timeless values of Ahimsa (non-violence), Seva (selfless service), and Karuna (compassion). At Panjarpol Poona Trust, we strive to: Rescue and Shelter: Save animals from slaughterhouses, abandonment, and abuse, offering them refuge and lifelong care. Medical Care: Provide regular health checkups, emergency treatments, and specialized veterinary care for sick, injured, or aging animals. Sustainable Living: Promote organic farming, cow-based products like jaivik-khat, gomutra, etc. and sustainable environmental practices. Education & Awareness: Conduct workshops, school visits, and community programs to educate people about animal welfare, cow protection, and ecological balance. Spiritual Connection: Uphold and spread the spiritual significance of the cow in Indian culture through Gau Seva, Gau Puja, and religious ceremonies. Every life matters. Our mission is to ensure that no animal suffers due to neglect, cruelty, or ignorance — and to build a world where compassion guides human actions.
